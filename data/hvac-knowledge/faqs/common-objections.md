# FAQ: Top 10 Objections + Responses

> Sample objection-handling scripts grounded in pricing/SOPs. The AI must cite the source
> doc it pulls the supporting fact from (e.g. pricing or financing).

## 1. "That's too expensive."
Acknowledge → reframe on value → offer financing.
> "I hear you. The diagnostic is just $89 and it's waived if you book the repair the same
> visit, so you're not paying twice [Source: pricing/ac-repair-2024.md § Diagnostic Fee].
> For bigger repairs we also have 0% financing for 12 months [Source: pricing/ac-repair-2024.md § Financing].
> Want me to get a tech out to give you the exact number?"

## 2. "I need to think about it."
Lower the commitment, add mild urgency.
> "Totally fair. The visit itself doesn't commit you to the repair — the tech just diagnoses
> it and gives you the exact price, then it's your call. I've got a slot today; want me to
> hold it so you're not waiting days in this heat?"

## 3. "Can you come today?"
Check calendar, offer real slots.
> "Let me check today's availability… I have [slot A] or [slot B]. Which works?"
> (Availability comes from the GoHighLevel calendar, never invented.)

## 4. "I'll just get another quote first."
> "Smart to compare. Our diagnostic gives you an itemized, exact quote you can compare
> apples-to-apples — and it's waived if you move forward with us. Want me to book it?"

## 5. "Why is there a diagnostic fee?"
> "It covers the tech's time to properly diagnose the issue instead of guessing. And it's
> waived if you book the repair on that same visit [Source: pricing/ac-repair-2024.md § Diagnostic Fee]."

## 6. "Is this covered under warranty?"
> "Depends on when it was installed — let me check. If we installed it, repairs carry a
> 90-day labor warranty and parts are often 5–10 years [Source: sops/warranty-policy.md].
> Let me book a quick warranty check so we confirm before you pay anything."

## 7. "My last company ripped me off."
Empathize → proof → low-risk next step.
> "I'm sorry that happened. Everything we quote is in writing and the diagnostic is only $89.
> No surprise charges — the tech confirms the price before any work starts."

## 8. "Do you do free estimates?"
> "For new-system installs, yes — the consult is free. For repairs there's an $89 diagnostic
> that's waived if you book the repair same visit [Source: pricing/ac-repair-2024.md]."

## 9. "It's making a weird noise but still works."
> "Better to catch it early — a small part now is cheaper than a compressor later. A tune-up
> starts at $99 and the tech will pinpoint the noise [Source: pricing/ac-repair-2024.md § Maintenance / Tune-Up]."

## 10. "How much for a whole new AC?"
> "Installs vary by size and efficiency, so we do a free in-home consult to size it right and
> give you an exact quote with financing options [Source: services/ac-installation.md].
> Want me to set that up?"
